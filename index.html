The King of AI - Google

October 13, 2024 by Rivaan Ranawat

Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin. It was initially started as a search engine named Backrub and later on changed their named to Google (thank you!). Search engine remains their core product but the company has expanded ever since. They have more than half a dozen products with over 1 billion users. All of these products have been a great contributor to changing people's lives - Google Maps changed the way we navigate, YouTube changed the way we consume content, Google Search changed the way we access data! What's interesting is a simple clone of these products is not easy to build even now. Think of building Google Maps - how would you go about it? You need to have lots of data about roads (and initially to collect this data, Google vans/cars used to roam around the city), you need routing algorithms, you need traffic information. All of this required research. Even the products that don't have a billion useers, like Google Translate have so much research done behind the scenes. The fun fact is Google Translate (today) uses Transformer architecture behind the scenes - the same architecture used by ChatGPT (GPT stands for Generate Pretrained Tranformer). This Transformer architecture was also introduced by researchers at Google! So, yes, ChatGPT exists because of Google. Think about the great work they're still doing as they invest more heavily in AI/ML. Their projects which are not as famous are going to change the world forever, like Waymo! Truly, the king of AI Research.

The Strong Base of AI - Nvidia

October 13, 2024 by Rivaan Ranawat

Nvidia was founded by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachaowsky and Curtis Priem in 1993. From 1993 to 1999, they did great work, no doubt. They sold more than a million units, introducing processors to render 2D and 3D images but I believe their greatest hit and what decided their future trajectory was in 1999, when they introduced the world to the first GPU ever - GeForce 256 (and also went public at 12$ per share). The company has been redefining themselves ever since despite "just" making GPUs. They literally created an industry which is the hottest thing of AI right now. GPUs are in demand so much that NVIDIA (legally, it is NVIDIA, not Nvidia) is a $3.3T company despite a P/E ratio of 63.26 and only 30,000 employees (flex, I was one of them (intern)). This might seem like an extremely hyped company and maybe it is but I think there is good reasoning behind this - GPUs are really good at matrix multiplication - They are very fast. They allow programmers to code in that general purpose GPU using CUDA. And that's what AI is about, matrix multiplication and allowing engineers to have control over their GPUs so that they can efficiently use it. No wonder, Nvidia is a go-to for all the major big name tech corporations right now - Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, Tesla, xAI, Oracle. Each of these GPUs cost more than $30,000 and recently Elon Musk announced the completion of building a supercompute cluster using 100,000 of the GPUs (H100) in 4 months. Think about it - $3B revenue for Nvidia in 4 months. According to reports, Microsoft is the biggest client of Nvidia. And Microsoft owns about 49% of OpenAI - That's why I say Nvidia is the base of all AI that's happening! Now, for how long that's going to remain, I don't know. Tesla, I know is building their own chip, Amazon too. Google has TPUs which is better at inferencing (getting conclusion) and probably even training than a GPU but hardware business is so tough - it's going to take some time until someone really replaces Nvidia as they even try to lock the companies in their ecosystem and build data centers of their own, knowingly or unknowingly competing with AWS (i guess), Amazon's biggest source of revenue. AMD looks good too but they're playing a catchup game and Intel is just out but will be interesting to see if they make a comeback.
